Privacy Policy

Orbit Down Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 10, 2026

Orbit Down is designed to be free, ad-free, private, and useful without an account. This policy explains what the app stores, what it does not collect, and how optional Apple services are used.

Privacy Summary

No tracking Orbit Down does not track you across apps or websites.
No ads The app does not include advertising SDKs or ad networks.
No analytics The app does not include an analytics SDK or developer-operated analytics service.
No account You do not need an Orbit Down account to use the app.

Data Stored By The App

Orbit Down stores the countdowns, collections, tags, notes, symbols, colors, widget preferences, reminders, completion history, templates, palettes, backups, and optional photo background references that you create. This data is stored on your device by default.

If you enable iCloud sync, each countdown, saved palette, and saved template is stored as an individual record in your private Apple CloudKit database, and selected photo backgrounds are stored as private CloudKit assets. For one release, legacy iCloud Key-Value Storage and iCloud Drive data is read only as migration input. The developer does not operate an account system or backend service and does not collect your synced content.

Apple Permissions

  • Calendar: Used only when you import events as countdowns or add countdown targets back to Calendar.
  • Reminders: Used only when you import dated reminders or add countdown targets to Reminders.
  • Contacts: Used only when you import birthdays or anniversaries.
  • Notifications: Used to schedule reminder notifications you configure.
  • Photos: Used only when you choose a photo background for a countdown or widget.

Backups And Sharing

Orbit Down can export backups, portable countdown files, calendar invite text, Markdown summaries, plain-text summaries, QR codes, and share-card images when you choose those actions. Exported files are controlled by you and the destination you select.

Deleting Data

You can delete countdowns, clear completion history, remove local data from Settings, and delete exported backups from wherever you saved them. If iCloud sync is enabled, Apple sync behavior may affect how deletion propagates across your signed-in devices.
